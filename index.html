Unfinished business

We still need to decide whether the first release should support interactive seating selection only or also live seat availability updates for users.

We also deferred a decision on whether notifications should be simple in-browser reminders or more noticeable audio alerts by default.

New business

The team agreed to prototype a minimal stadium seating interface with different sections and rows. We discussed adding a stadium map later, and noted that accessibility for wheelchair seating and navigation should be considered from the first prototype onward.

We captured a rough component outline on the whiteboard: a seat selection engine, a small UI layer for controls, and a lightweight settings store for section, row, and seat preferences.

Miscellaneous comments, questions, and concerns

Notes from open discussion

Several teammates asked whether we should require a full stadium map in version one. The group preferred keeping the first milestone smaller unless a stadium clearly needs more advanced interaction.

Concern raised: avoid scope creep from "nice-to-have" features before the core seating experience is stable.
Follow-up question: do we want analytics on seat selection behavior, or is that out of scope for the project?
